Gloucester Serpent

Gloucester, Massachusetts, United States

  • Class: Aquatic/Semi-aquatic
  • Earliest sighting was in 1638 by John Josselyn off Cape Ann
  • Witnesses have reported seeing a spear in the head of the creature. Many beleive it to be a narwhal.

Description:

  • About 60 feet long
  • Massive serpent-like Creature
  • Humps all along the back

Montauk Monster

Montauk, New York, United States

  • Discovered as a caracass on the beach
  • Believed to have actually been a water-degraded raccoon, a sea turtle, a rodent, dog, or sheep. All these possibilites were denied by William Wise (Director of Stony Brook University's Living Marine Resources Institute)
  • Other Names: omajinaakoos (the Ugly One)

Description:

  • Small and hairless
  • A beak-like snout and sharp teeth
  • Bloated, and water-logged

Dover Demon

  • About 4 feet tall
